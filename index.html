Prophet Muhammad

Muhammad is the founding figure of Islam.

About Muhammad

  • Mother dreamed of his birth that he was a light that left her body and lit up the palaces of Sham.
  • Was born in Mecca, Saudi Arabia on the 22nd of April, 571 CE, The Year of the Elephant.
  • Descendant of Prophet Ismail, son of Prophet Ibrahim.
  • Father died before his birth and mother died soon after his birth.
  • Had multiple wives during his lifetime.
  • Received his first revelation from Allah through Angel Gabriel in Cave Hira during a voluntary stay in search of solitude.
  • After revelation, Prophet Muhammad began preaching Islam to people in small doses.
  • Prayed constantly.
  • Regularly invited kings to Islam.
  • Never claimed to be divine.
  • Unspoken rule: Visual depictions of Prophet Muhammad or Allah are forbidden.
  • Died after his last pilgrimage on the 8th of June, 632 CE of high fever, and was buried in the mosque in Medina.
  • The Muslims regard Muhammad as Allah's final messenger, known as the 'Seal of the Prophets'.
